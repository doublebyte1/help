INTRODUCTION

Medfisis is a package for the storage, management and processing of fisheries Census, sampling and Logbook, based on differently currently existing systems (like ArtFish, PescArte, Medfisis and others). The objective is to have a free and open-source solution, that can be used, maintained and customized in developing countries, on a zero-cost basis.

This user guide is composed by three main chapters. The first chapter focused on main features supported by Medfisis, giving details on information supported, how information is organised and  presented to the user, and how user navigates through out the interface forms to enter data.

The second chapter gives insight details on the process of data storage for both data sources, artisanal fisheries and logbooks. A set of screen-shots of the application had been introduced in order to facilitate a good comprehension of the contents.

OVERVIEW OF MEDFISIS 1.1

NATURE OF THE INFORMATION SUPPORTED BY MedFisis 1.1

Medfisis consider two standard sampling processes: logbook and artisanal fisheries. Basically it offers capability to store and manage two distinct types of fisheries information, census and sampling data, typified as logbooks and artisanal fisheries.

logbooks: Typically the data source for semi-industrial and industrial fisheries, consisting of reports submitted by companies, vessel’s legal representative (master, captain, owner, etc.) giving detailed information on individual fishing operations, including fishing grounds, type and duration of operation, fishing gears, catch by species, etc..

Current version of Medfisis, only supports information of catches organized by commercial category, i.e., by identifying group of species (e.g Small gregarious pelagic, Large pelagic, etc.). For further details about Commercial Category please consult the glossary.

Even though in theory the logbooks as a data source consists of a census approach, for several circumstances sometimes logbooks are not completely collected, resulting in incomplete and inaccurate fisheries information. To overcome such circumstances some methodologies for data raising can be applied. MedFisis supports the integration of such process to Manage Logbook Data.  Another important feature is the ability to store non census information for cross checking fishing logs.

Artisanal fisheries: Include all kind of fisheries to which information is collected with questionnaires, interviews or direct observations on the fishing vessels, at landing sites, processing plants, or in markets. Variables that enumerators can collect include catch (landing and discards), effort, vessel/gears, operations, biological variables (e.g. weight; length; sex, etc.), the values and quantities of landings and sales.

Along with information on effort and catch, MedFisis support information taken from biological sampling, like sample dimension - number of sampled individuals - and the average weight per individual. Further developments will allow the storage of additional variables coming from biological sampling, such as length, ages and sex.

THE SAMPLING PROCESS

The collection of fisheries data is in most situations an expensive and hard task to execute. Mainly due to the nature of the fishing activity (spread along the countries coast, ...) , but also for the lack of accuracy of the information that sometimes is provided as well as for the difficulty it may represents for the fishermen, mainly artisanal, to answer and fill in such complex forms to deliver data. Sampling approach is more cost-effective approach then total enumeration process and if sampling design is well tailored to the fishery it will produce accurate results.

On the other hand, the collection of 100% fishing reports, such as logbooks, is most of the times not reachable. If these data is not inspected a posteriori in order to overcome the possible problem of misrepresentation and inaccuracy it may lead to the production of biased and incomplete estimates.

MedFisis integrate the possibility to store and manage census data and sampling data, both for artisanal fisheries and logbook (industrial fisheries). Regarding sampling data, Medfisis 1.1 can store, manage and perform statistical analysis for sampling surveys based on random sampling and stratified random sampling.
Future developments of Medfisis will be able to store, manage and perform statistical analysis of sampling data collected based on more sophisticated methodologies , such as cluster sampling and multistage sampling.

However in order to enable analysis of sampling data, a complete description of the sampling process must be provided. This includes: identification of sampling design and storage of the metadata associated - like size of population, size of sample a description of the sample units, if stratified sampling used, also a description of the strata and the population and sample distribution per strata.

Navigation through Forms

The Database Module of Medfisis has an interface with eight main forms: Frame, Minor Strata, Cell, Vessel Type, Vessel, Fishing Trip, Fishing Operation and Catch. All forms have a similar design structure; a sub-form showing a list of records previously introduced, and a sub-form, "Details", where user is allowed to enter new records. The sub-form Details may be activated by simple selecting a record from the list, or by clicking the button "Create new record". By clicking the button Close, sub-form Details gets inactive and not visible, becoming only visible the list of records introduced. Each form within the Details sub-form has a comment window where user is invited to introduce the relevant information for a purpose of documentation.

For each record entered and showed in the list user is allowed to edit, modify and delete. For that, user must select the line containing the record and the buttons on the right side of the form will execute the needed order.

All forms support right-click context menus to commonly used commands for that view. All views support context help. Creating the Sampling frame

The entry point for data collection is acceded through the first form – FRAME. This form allows the definition of the Sampling Frame.

The sampling frame is the most basic structure to define the data collection process where characterizes both physically - set of vessels, grouped by landing sites (LS) and groups of landing sites (GLS) - and temporally - time validity - the scope of the data collection operation.

A sampling frame can be initialized either by choosing an existent one - copy from a pre-existent - or creating a new one from scratch.

Create a new frame from scratch:

A new frame is initialised by selecting in the sub-form Physical frame “Create New” and then “Create from Scratch”.  The button (...) will prompt to the definition widget, figure below,  where the geographical frame must be defined by selecting the vessels, landing sites, group of landing sites or even abstract landing site, that compose the frame.

Within the definition widget a list of elements are shown in a tree-like structure, with following levels:

"Location -> Group of Landing Sites -> Landing Sites -> Vessel".

Where "Location"  identifies the location of the frame pursuant it nature, i.e,  Root - if it's a active frame or Recycled- for inactive frames. The legend for each element presented in the sampling frame may be seen by selecting the proper button "Legend " in the bottom of the window. Along with he button "legend", the user may also find two buttons to expand and collapse the levels presented in the geographical frame.

User may add or delete levels of the tree-like structure by right clicking on the top of the selected item. Each element may be edit by call F2 when in top of the selected element.

Define the time validity

After concluding the geographical characterization, user is prompt again to the first frame screen to define the time validity.

Time validity is the period for which data has been collected. In some situation differences may occur between the period of data collection and the period to which data refers to. In this situation, to assure the correctness of posterior procedures, the period to which data refers shall be adopted.

To set time validity a starting date and end date must be entered in the sub-form Details/Time Frame.  Note the time period here defined will constitute a constraint to all subsequent data entries of the posterior levels.

Along with choosing a physical frame, and determining its validity, the sampling frame gets defined with the characterization of the sampling process.

Define the sampling process

The correct description of the sampling procedures is crucial for the posterior procedures to be applied to  data in order to get final estimates, such as (Total catch; total effort; CPUE; etc.). The identification of the sampling methodologies will constraint the algorithms to be applied to sample observations as well as the correct description of the sampling process 

To give a complete description of the sampling process the following information needs to be collected. we also want to collect some more information about the sampling process itself.

Global strategy – identify the overall sampling methodology applied in the survey design - Random sampling, stratified random sampling.

Sample Size – Number of elements to be taken from the population;

Population Size – dimension of the population.

If the sampling methodology is Stratified Random Sampling, then it is also need to identify sample size by strata (ni), as well as population size per each strata Ni, each strata will correspond to one Level.

Define Minor Strata

The Minor Strata is defined by a Group of landing sites together with a time validity. It represents the first level of  to characterise the sampling process being the way of aggregate information by major geographical location.

To Create New Minor Strata (MS), first of all user must enter the designation of the new Minor Strata and then enter the Minor Strata information within the Details window. By default MedFisis set as designation "New Minor Strata".  

To create a new Minor Strata user needs to select a Group of Landing Site (GLS) from scroll-list where all the GLS belonging to the Physical  frame will be shown and then characterise it activity status. The Activity Status of a GLS characterise either the fishing activity, if active or not active, or the type of answer coming from that GLS, i.e., if logbooks had been filled in.

Along with GLS user must identify the period for which information has been collected in that given GLS. The time validity is defined by identifying the start and end date. Note that dates must be comprised within temporal validity of the sampling frame.

Define the Cell Level

The Cell  represents the level of the landing site (LS). Cell level only has relevance for the description of the sampling process for artisanal fisheries since it collects the relevant information to general describe the sampled vessels in each landing site. 


Therefore user needs to give details on the number of vessels observed, from these, the ones that are active, inactive  and on vessels observed in the LS coming from a different LS. In this level user is allowed to setup any temporary change to the frame initially defined, for that before move to next level it's mandatory the inspection of the elements identified on the geographical frame. Thus after to complete all fields to create the New Cell, user must navigate to the definition form by selecting the Button (...) in the tool-bar right side of the form.  


Fields requested in Details sub-form:


Number of vessels - number of vessels belonging to landing site with importance for the sampling purpose

Number of active vessel - subset of active vessels within the total number of vessels with fishing activity during the reference period

Number inactive vessels - subset of inactive vessels within the total number of vessels without fishing activity during the reference period

Number of vessels from outside - vessels with fishing activity in the landing site cell but originally registered in some other landing site.

Start date and end date - define the period during which observation had been taken from that particular landing site. Along with dates and whenever relevant, and known, user may tick on the "has time" box, enabling the field for the collection of the time at start and at the end of the sampling at the landing site.  


The comments windowallow user to add some additional information with relevance for the good comprehension of the sampling process namely for the level where data has been input.


identify the Vessels Type

Together with the Cell Level, the level of the Vessel Type only has relevance - therefore it only gets activate - for the definition of the sampling process for the artisanal fisheries.
 Within this form user shall provide further details about the vessels selected in each landing site, now by characterising the number of vessels observed per each vessel type.  Further information please consult Vessel type.



As in previous forms, Vessel type  shows a list of the vessel types already created for the group "Sampling Frame -> Minor Strata -> Cell" that user is entering, and a sub-form "Details" to enter new values for other "vessel type".

Scroll list Vessel type shows the  vessel types identified for the current Landing Site at the Cell level. 

As for Cell level, user must enter the number of vessels observed, from those the ones with activity and those without activity and the number of vessels observed in current Landing Site that originally belong to another Landing Site. For a complete description of each field please consult "Cell Level - Sub-form Details" Note the sum of vessels identified in each tab, n vessels, active vessels, inactive vessels and outside vessels must fit the numbers identified on the cell level.

Identify the Vessels

Form Vessel allows the user to identify the vessels for which information has been collected and is going to be entered on the following levels, whether it refer to sampled vessels - artisanal fisheries vessels - or vessels sending regular information via logbooks.

 



As previous forms, the form Vessel is composed by two main sub-forms, one showing a list of the vessels already identified for current group "Frame->Minor Strata ->Cell->Vessel Type",  and a details sub-form where new vessels may be inserted.

Sub-form details:

Vessel - Scroll list with the identification of the vessels belonging to the frame that fulfils the criteria of previous levels, i.e, belong to the Landing Site and to the Vessel type previously selected.

Sample origin -  Sample origin characterises the process of data collection by allowing the user to select how information had been collected ( by a sampler;  sent by post; delivered to fishery office; not known, etc.).  If  this information is not known user may select n/a for not available or unknown.

Status - status filed characterises the information regarding it quality and level of completeness.  For further details please consult "Vessel - Status".

Number of fishing trips - identify the number of fishing trips performed by the selected vessel. Information on fishing trips for the sampling process  needs to correspond to fishing trips with catch observations in order to assure correctness of future estimates.

Enter the Fishing trip

With the initialisation of the Fishing trip level, user has finalised the introduction of Metadata (data to characterise data been collected), and is prompt to a form where fisheries data starts to be entered.
The form is composed by two main sub-forms, one showing a list of the fishing trips previously entered and the sub-form "Details" where user is enabled to enter a new  fishing trip.
 For each fishing trip, one need to provide information on trip duration, crew, fishing operations, gears used, fishing zones and total catch. 



Details window:

Sampler - Identify the person in charge for the collection of the sampling form. Usually for the logbooks this kind of information is not relevant, if so please choose  n/a.

Landing site - Identification of the landing site where vessels has landed. The identification of the Landing site is critical for logbooks, since for this data source is the first level LS are identified. For the artisanal fisheries Landing Sites must be in line with the ones chosen on the level cell .  Attention should be paid for the  differences between both type of frames.

N. of professional fishermen - Number of fishermen belonging to the crew that have as main professional activity the fisheries;

N. of part-time fishermen - Number of fishermen for which the fisheries represents a secondary activity or for which the fisheries is not a full time activity. 

Start Date - Date when trip has begun. Whenever possible and known, information about the time must be collected in order to enable posterior calculations on fishing effort. To activate the field to collect time, please add a tick to the box "Has time". 

End Date - The ending date of the trip.

Number of operations - Number of fishing operations performed during the fishing trip regardless of the gear/gears used.

Catch - total catch taken during the complete fishing trip. If total amount is expressed in kg, the  field catch shall be selected to input data, otherwise Catch by Box or Catch by units.

Catch by box - field to input catch expressed in number of boxes and average weight per box.

Catch by units - When other bin than box had been used, information on catch shall be input in the field catch by unit along with average weight by unit.

Gears used - Fishing gears deployed during the fishing trip. If more than one gear had been used, then a multiple selection shall be performed where all the fishing gears must be included. On the following level, "Fishing Operation",The gears selected within thee fishing trip will be the ones available to introduce fishing operation data.

Fishing zone - Fishing area(s) where vessel has deployed fishing effort. If more than one fishing area, than a multiple selection must be performed.

Characterise the Fishing operation

Fishing Operation tab collects details about each fishing operation, namely data on the effort deployed by gear, gear characteristics and total catch per each fishing operation/gear. The Fishing operation form is composed by two main sub-forms: a sub-form showing the list of the fishing operations previously introduced and a sub-form "Details" which enables the user to introduce new fishing operations.

Sub-form Details:

Order Number - Identifies the order of the fishing operation. The order number needs to be introduced by the user and must identify the order for which the fishing operations took place.

Fishing zone - Identifies the fishing area where the fishing operation took place. If the fishing operation took place in more then one fishing zone, user must identify the one that correspond to the larger duration. 

Start and End Date -  The identification of the moment where operation has started and has finished. To enable future estimation of effort variables, the information about the time of beginning and term of fishing operation needs to be entry.    
Gear - Identification of the fishing gear used during fishing operation. details
Units - Units and size are the metrics collected to characterise the fishing gear, where:
Unit - Identifies the number of devices deployed for passive fishing gear such as: trammel nets, gilnets, longlines, etc. If mobile gears had been deployed, than the field units must be used to collect data on 
Size - as well as field unit, Size vary with the type of gear in use. For passive gears, size identify the length of each unit of passive gear that had been set, e.g 3 trammel nets; For mobile gears - seine and trawl, the field size collects the information about the mesh size. 
Catch - Total catch resulting from the fishing operation. Note, the sum of the catch for each fishing operation must be equal to the total catch added for the fishing trip. catch can be inserted as a total expressed in kg, or if organised by standard bins, the following must be chosen:
catch by box - field to input catch expressed in number of boxes and average weight per box.
catch by units - When other bin than box had been used, information on catch shall be input in the field catch by unit along with average weight by unit.
Commercial category - The commercial category identifies the specific composition of the catch, not identifing each specie but identifying the major group of species (small pelagics, demersal species, etc.). For further details please consult Commercial Category.


Enter the Catch Data

The Catch tab, represents the last level for data entering. Current version of MedFisis only allow the identification the catch by commercial category, both for the catch and sample data.
As for previous forms, Catch form shows a list of the commercial categories introduced before and a sub-form where user can enter new records. The list of Commercial categories that will appear on the scroll list is confined to the ones identified on the previous level, Fishing Operation.
Details window - information for each commercial category prior identified

Totals -total catch taken from the commercial category. As well as for previous screens, information can be filled either by entering total catch or by using information by box or units.
Sub-form Sample, collects information about possible sampling taken from the commercial category identified. Information about number of individuals sampled  and average weight of sampled individual. Along with of the sample dimension and the average weight per individual, user needs to identify the weight units used (kg, g).
